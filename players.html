Top 5 Laker Players of the 2025 - 2026 season!


#23

LeBron James

LeBron Raymone James was born in Akron Ohio in 1984, making him 41 years old. Nicknamed King James, he is the NBA's all-time leading scorer and has won four NBA championships in 10 NBA Finals appearances. Along with this, he's won three Olymic gold medals as a member of the U.S. national team in 2008, 2012, and 2024. James is widely considered to be the greatest basketball player of all time.

#28

Rui Hachimura

Rui Hachimura was born Toyama, Japan in 1998, making him 28 years old. While in Japan, Hachimura achieved success at the youth level in Japan, leading Meisei High School to three straight All-Japan High School Tournament titles and being a top player for the Japanese under-17 national FIBA competition.

#15

Austin Reaves

Austin Reaves was born in Newark, Arkansas in 1998, making him 27 years old. Nicknamed Hill-billy Kobe, Austin played college basketball for the Oaklahoma Sooners and Wichita State Shockers before joined the Lakers as an undrafted free agent.

#5

Deandre Ayton

Deandre Ayton was born in Nassau, The Bahamas, in 1998, making him 27 years old. A consensus five-star prospect of the NBA Class of 2017 and an McDonald's All-American, Ayton played one season for the Arizona Wildcats before being selected as the first overall pick in the 2018 NBA draft by the Phoenix Suns. Although inconsistent at times, he remains a fitting piece to this 2025-2026 Lakers lineup.

#77

Luka Dončić

Luka Dončić was born in Ljubljana, Slovenia, in 1999, making him 26 years old. Nicknamed Luka Magic, he is broadly regarded as one of the greatest European players of all time. Since his rookie season, he has been selected to six NBA All-Star games and five All-NBA First Team selections. As part of one of the most controversial trades in NBA history, sending Luka from the Dallas Mavericks to the Lakers, Luka has not shined away from the LA lights since his forthcoming.
